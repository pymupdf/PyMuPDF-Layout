FAA Chart Users' Guide - VFR Symbology - Sectional and Terminal Area Charts

This image shows various airspace symbols and markings used on FAA VFR sectional and terminal area charts. It illustrates Special Flight Rules Areas (SFRA) marked with blue boundaries, Special Use Airspace areas like prohibited/restricted zones and military operations areas shown with different colored hatching patterns, and Special Air Traffic Rules areas marked with diagonal blue lines. The symbols help pilots identify different types of controlled or restricted airspace with specific operating requirements.

## Flight Restricted Zone (FRZ) Relating to
National Security

| Area Type | Symbol/Representation | Description/Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Flight Restricted Zone | Blue line with hatching into a gray shaded area, labeled "FLIGHT RESTRICTED ZONE". | |
| National Security Area | Thick dashed purple line forming a boundary. | Appropriate notes as required may be shown. Example notice: "NOTICE FOR REASONS OF NATIONAL SECURITY PILOTS ARE REQUESTED TO AVOID FLIGHT BELOW 1200' MSL IN THIS AREA" |
| Special Awareness Training Areas | Solid blue line within a gray shaded area, labeled (e.g., "DCA-VOR-DME-60-NM"). | Example notice: "NOTICE Special awareness training required within 60 NM of DCA VOR-DME. See description on Flyway." |
| Mode C (FAR 91.215) | Solid magenta arc, labeled "MODE C" and with a distance (e.g., "30 NM"). | Appropriate notes as required may be shown. |
| Air Defense Identification Zone (ADIZ) | Line of magenta dots, labeled (e.g., "CONTIGUOUS U.S. ADIZ"). | Note: Delimiting line not shown when it coincides with International Boundary, projection lines or other linear features. |
